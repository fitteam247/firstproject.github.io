Rio Carnival

is a street festival with music and Samba dance in Brazil. Rhythm, participation, and costumes vary from one region of Brazil to another. The type of energy that the city of Rio emulates is just simply electrifying. What is even more exhilarating being the deliciously bronzed samba dancers, the pulsating music, the energy and applause of the spectators filled with Cariocas and visitors from around the world who call come to the same place to experience the world famous Rio Carnival

Samba music and dance

find their home in Brazil. To a Brazilian, Samba the dance, is a solo dance danced by Carnaval (Carnival) dancers. For ballroom dancers, Samba is a partner dance that many Brazilians will not recognize as a dance with Brazilian roots.

The Sambadrome Marques de Sapucai

is the purpose-built stadium located in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, designed specifically to host the annual parade of Samba Schools during Rio Carnival. The Sambodromo was designed and built in 1984 by world-renowned architect Oscar Niemeyer, from the early history of Rio Carnival to the more recent renovations in preparation for the Rio 2016 Olympic Games.

Allegorical cars

Allegorical cars help the carnival producer tell the plot-story within the parade and are a contest evaluation criterion. Some floats are extremely rich and they carry people, statues, and other luxurious sculptures. A float is a decorated platform, either built on a vehicle like a truck or towed behind one. The largest floats is about 35m entry and a float weighing more than 100,000 pounds.

sambodromo
tips

Food

The rules for bringing food to the Sambadrome are unlike most other entertainment venues around the world. You are permitted to bring two 50oml plastic beverage con- tainers and two food items, like fruits, sandwiches or other snacks.

Photography and Video

You are encouraged to take your memories back home with you. Therefore, you are permitted to take any kind of pictures or video of the parade for personal use only.

Dress code

There is no official set of rules with respect to a dress code for the Sambadrome. You are encouraged to dress like the local cariocas, but keep in mind that Rio Carnival takes place during the Rio de Janeiro summer, so it gets hot at this time of the year. Wearing light clothes is advised, especially if you are sitting in the grandstands.

Ticket pricing

No prices are guaranteed until paid for in full. Admissions grandstand seats are free for children under the age of 5. Prices generally get more expensive as the carnival dates get closer so you are advised to purchase tickets in advance.

Getting
to
sambodromo

Taxi

Our preferred way is to jump in a taxi. They're relatively cheap in Rio and makes things easier. Once out the taxi, follow the crowds a short 10 minute walk towards to Sambadrome.

Subway - Metro Rio

Arriving to the Sambodromo by subway during Rio Carnival is both convenient and cheap. Metro Rio runs 24 hours a day during Carnival, so when the parade ends at 5 or 6 in the morning, rest assured, the subway will be running. Trains depart from the stations every ten minutes.
